AI4ALL Decision Platform

A participatory land-cover change visualisation platform that makes decades of satellite data legible to non-experts. Citizens and urban planners compare historical land cover side-by-side on an interactive Leaflet map.

Overview+

Making land change visible to everyone

The AI4ALL project asks a fundamental question: how do you include citizens in decisions about their environment when the data that drives those decisions is locked behind technical interfaces?

This platform turns OpenLandMap satellite data into an interactive split-screen comparison — anyone can drag a divider to reveal how land cover has changed between any two years from 2000 to 2023.

Two modes serve two audiences: Data mode for analysts (raw land-cover tiles, opacity controls, quantified legend) and Story mode for citizens (narrative overlays that contextualise what the map is showing).

Interface Modes+

Data mode vs Story mode

🛰

Data Mode

Raw land-cover tiles. Year comparison slider. Opacity control. Quantified legend with land class breakdown.

🌱

Story Mode

Narrative overlays replace technical UI. Real IGN aerial imagery. LoupeLens magnification for community zoom-ins.

↔

Split Compare

Leaflet Side-by-Side control: drag the vertical divider to compare any two years of land-cover data.

◉

Ideology Panel

Contextual information explaining the participatory decision-making thesis underlying the visualisation.
