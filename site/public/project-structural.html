Overview+

Structural intelligence at concept stage

Structural analysis traditionally enters the architectural workflow too late — after design decisions are locked in. Grounded brings finite element analysis to the earliest design phase, when changes are cheapest and most impactful.

Architects upload a rough 3D model (GLB or OBJ) of orthogonal volumes. The tool automatically detects structural elements, places columns using perimeter-first logic, and runs PyNite FEA to estimate load paths and flag structural weaknesses.

A multi-model RAG layer then queries structural design codes and best practices to provide actionable recommendations alongside the quantitative FEA results.

Pipeline+

Automated analysis workflow

01

Model Upload

Upload .glb or .obj. Trimesh parses the geometry into a raw mesh for processing.

02

Mesh Segmentation

Walls, floors, and roofs are automatically detected and separated by geometry orientation and area thresholds.

03

Structural Grid

A custom algorithm generates a structural grid based on floorplate geometry and wall intersections, using perimeter-first column logic.

04

FEA Report

PyNite runs finite element analysis, outputting displacement maps, reaction forces, and structural warnings.
