## Project Breakdown

### P.01: Floating Self Sufficient Community
**Location**: Lakshadweep, India  
**Type**: Undergraduate Thesis / Master's Research  
**Years**: Thesis Project  
**Client**: Academic / Research  
**Status**: Conceptual / Realized Design  

**Description**:
Ambitious climate-responsive floating city designed to rehabilitate island populations from Lakshadweep following rising sea levels. Features hexagonal modular structures using GFRC, lightweight steel gauge framing, and computer-generated mesh distribution systems. Aerodynamic analysis via SIMSCALE. Eco-mooring with biorock wireframes for coral regeneration.

**Key Features**:
- Modular hexagonal units for mass scaling
- GFRC + lightweight steel construction
- Wind tunnel analysis (SIMSCALE)
- Eco-mooring with biorock integration
- Coral regeneration focus

### P.04: Large-Scale IT Park — Chennai
**Location**: Chennai, India  
**Type**: IT Park / Commercial Campus  
**Site Area**: 12.67 acres  
**Orientation**: Southwest-facing (challenging)  
**Years**: 2022–2024  
**Firm**: Morphogenesis  
**Status**: Realized/Constructed  

**Description**:
Large-scale IT park on southwest-facing site requiring active thermal management. Staggered massing minimizes solar exposure. Self-shading fins provide passive climate control. Intermediate greenscapes across floors and ground. Seamless pedestrian circulation. Massive central atrium provides natural light and ventilation.

**Key Features**:
- 12.67-acre campus
- Southwest-facing (thermal challenge)
- Staggered massing strategy
- Self-shading fins (passive cooling)
- Multi-level greenscapes
- Central atrium (natural ventilation)
- Pedestrian-focused design

**Design Strategies**:
- Light penetration analysis with atrium
- Color variation in facade elements
- Interior lobby with wooden decks
- Urban streetscape integration
- Boulevard design
