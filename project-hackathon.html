Overview

Urban development meets simulation, meets play

City growth models are notoriously opaque — driven by equations planners and citizens can't interpret. The ZONO_NAUTS challenge was: how do you make urban prediction legible?

The answer was two-fold. First, a Graph Neural Network trained on Barcelona's urban grid to predict zoning transitions, land use dominance, and new development zones — patterns invisible to traditional analysis.

Second, a Minecraft-style voxelisation pipeline that converts the city grid into a spatial format anyone can explore. Urban development becomes a world you can walk through, not a chart you can't read.

The project opens real questions: how do planners, developers, and citizens participate in shaping the cities the model predicts?

Workflow

Modular pipeline

01
OSMnx Extraction
Building footprints, land use polygons, and POIs extracted from OpenStreetMap for any target city.
02
Grid-to-Graph
A 250×250m urban grid is generated then converted to a graph format for GNN training, preserving spatial relationships.
03
GNN Training
Graph Neural Network predicts zoning transitions and land use dominance across urban nodes.
04
Voxelisation
3D city blocks converted to .schematic format — making urban predictions interactive and spatially immersive.
